# OctoAcme Personas

This document defines typical roles and responsibilities used in OctoAcme project docs and exercises.

---

## Developers

### Role Summary
Developers design, build, test, and deliver software components. They collaborate with product and project leads to implement features that meet acceptance criteria and quality standards.

### Responsibilities
- Implement features and fixes to meet acceptance criteria
- Write and maintain tests and documentation
- Participate in design and code reviews
- Assist in estimating and planning work
- Help identify technical risks and propose mitigations

### Goals
- Deliver reliable, maintainable code
- Reduce cycle time from idea to production
- Maintain high test coverage and observability

### Typical Communication
- Daily standups and sprint planning
- PR descriptions and code review comments
- Technical design docs when needed

---

## Product Managers

### Role Summary
Product Managers define what should be built to deliver customer and business value. They own the product vision, prioritize the backlog, and measure outcomes.

### Responsibilities
- Define problem statements and success metrics
- Prioritize the roadmap and backlog
- Collaborate with stakeholders and engineering on trade-offs
- Validate solutions through user research and metrics

### Goals
- Maximize customer value and impact
- Make clear, data-driven prioritization decisions
- Ensure product-market fit and usability

### Typical Communication
- Weekly alignment with PM and engineering leads
- Roadmap updates and stakeholder briefings
- Acceptance criteria and feature specs

---

## Project Managers

### Role Summary
Project Managers coordinate delivery activities, manage schedules, risks, and communications. They enable the team to deliver on commitments efficiently.

### Responsibilities
- Create and maintain project plans and timelines
- Manage risks, dependencies, and resource constraints
- Facilitate meetings (kickoff, planning, retrospectives)
- Ensure consistent project documentation and status reporting
- Coordinate cross-team and stakeholder communication

### Goals
- Deliver projects on time and within scope
- Minimize unplanned work and escalations
- Maintain transparency and alignment across stakeholders

### Typical Communication
- Weekly status updates and stakeholder reports
- Risk registers and decision logs
- Coordination via project boards and meeting facilitation

---

## Release Manager

### Role Summary
Release Managers coordinate and oversee all release activities from planning through deployment. They ensure deployment checklists are followed, timelines are communicated to stakeholders, and manage incident response during problematic deployments.

### Responsibilities
- Coordinate release schedules and deployment windows
- Ensure all pre-release requirements are met (CI passing, security scans, release notes)
- Manage deployment checklist execution and track deployment progress
- Communicate release timelines and status to stakeholders
- Coordinate rollback procedures and incident response for failed deployments
- Maintain release documentation and post-mortems
- Track and report on deployment metrics and success rates

### Goals
- Deliver reliable, predictable releases with minimal downtime
- Reduce deployment-related incidents and improve mean time to recovery
- Maintain clear communication and visibility throughout the release process

### Typical Communication
- Pre-release planning meetings with Developers, QA, and DevOps
- Deployment status updates to Project Managers and stakeholders
- Post-deployment reports and retrospectives
- Incident coordination during deployment issues
- Collaboration with DevOps Engineers on deployment automation
- Release readiness reviews with QA Engineers

---

## QA Engineer

### Role Summary
QA Engineers design, implement, and execute comprehensive test plans to ensure software quality. They are responsible for verifying acceptance criteria, tracking defects, and maintaining the quality gate before releases.

### Responsibilities
- Design test plans and test cases based on acceptance criteria
- Execute manual and automated testing (unit, integration, end-to-end)
- Track, report, and verify defects through resolution
- Maintain and expand test automation coverage
- Validate features against acceptance criteria and user stories
- Perform regression testing before releases
- Provide quality metrics and testing status reports
- Participate in planning to identify testability requirements

### Goals
- Prevent defects from reaching production
- Increase test automation coverage and efficiency
- Reduce cycle time for testing while maintaining quality standards
- Improve early detection of issues in the development cycle

### Typical Communication
- Daily standups to report testing progress and blockers
- Test plan reviews with Developers and Product Managers
- Defect triage meetings with Developers
- Release readiness sign-off with Release Manager
- Acceptance testing collaboration with Product Managers
- Test automation strategy discussions with Developers and DevOps

---

## UX Designer

### Role Summary
UX Designers advocate for user needs and ensure consistent, intuitive user experiences. They deliver wireframes, mockups, and prototypes while working closely with Product and Engineering to balance usability with technical constraints.

### Responsibilities
- Conduct user research and usability testing
- Create wireframes, mockups, and interactive prototypes
- Ensure consistency in user flows, visual design, and interaction patterns
- Collaborate on acceptance criteria to include usability requirements
- Participate in design reviews and provide feedback on implementations
- Maintain design systems and component libraries
- Advocate for accessibility and inclusive design practices

### Goals
- Deliver intuitive, accessible user experiences
- Reduce user friction and support tickets related to usability
- Maintain design consistency across the product
- Balance user needs with technical feasibility and business constraints

### Typical Communication
- Design reviews with Product Managers and Developers
- User research findings presentations to stakeholders
- Design handoff sessions with Developers
- Usability testing sessions and results sharing
- Design critique sessions with peers
- Sprint planning to ensure design readiness for upcoming work

---

## Customer Success Manager

### Role Summary
Customer Success Managers act as the bridge between the project team and end users. They ensure smooth product adoption, gather feedback, communicate user needs back to the team, and help drive customer satisfaction and retention.

### Responsibilities
- Onboard new customers and ensure successful adoption
- Gather and synthesize customer feedback and feature requests
- Communicate issues, bugs, and enhancement requests to the project team
- Track customer satisfaction metrics and identify improvement areas
- Provide product education and best practices to customers
- Escalate customer-impacting issues to appropriate teams
- Represent customer perspective in planning and prioritization discussions

### Goals
- Maximize customer satisfaction and product adoption
- Reduce churn and increase retention
- Create a feedback loop that informs product improvements
- Ensure customers achieve their desired outcomes with the product

### Typical Communication
- Customer feedback sessions and quarterly business reviews
- Weekly updates to Product Managers on customer trends and requests
- Collaboration with QA on customer-reported issues
- Escalation coordination with Project Managers for critical issues
- Product roadmap discussions to align with customer needs
- Support ticket reviews and trend analysis with the team

---

## DevOps Engineer

### Role Summary
DevOps Engineers implement and maintain the infrastructure, CI/CD pipelines, and automation that enable reliable, efficient software delivery. They bridge development and operations, focusing on automation, monitoring, and system reliability.

### Responsibilities
- Design, implement, and maintain CI/CD pipelines
- Manage infrastructure as code (IaC) and configuration management
- Monitor system health, performance, and availability
- Implement automated deployments and rollback mechanisms
- Ensure security best practices in infrastructure and deployment
- Support incident response and troubleshooting
- Optimize build and deployment processes for speed and reliability
- Maintain documentation for infrastructure and deployment procedures

### Goals
- Maximize deployment frequency and reliability
- Minimize mean time to recovery for incidents
- Maintain high system availability and performance
- Reduce manual toil through automation

### Typical Communication
- CI/CD planning and optimization with Developers
- Deployment coordination with Release Manager
- Infrastructure planning with Project and Product Managers
- Incident response collaboration with on-call teams
- Security reviews with security team
- Post-incident retrospectives and improvement planning
